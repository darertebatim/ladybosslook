## Module 4: Cross-Cultural Communication

### Understanding Cultural Differences

**Key Differences in Business Culture:**

| Aspect | Western (US/Europe) | Middle Eastern/Iranian |
|--------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Time** | Punctual, time-focused | More flexible |
| **Communication** | Direct, explicit | Indirect, context-based |
| **Hierarchy** | Flat structures | Respect for hierarchy |
| **Negotiation** | Fast-paced | Relationship-building first |
| **Decision-Making** | Individual/quick | Collective/thoughtful |

### Cultural Intelligence Tips

1. **Research the culture** before important meetings
2. **Observe and adapt** your communication style
3. **Ask questions** when unsure about customs
4. **Show respect** for cultural traditions
5. **Be patient** with different pacing

## Module 5: Mastering Code-Switching

### What is Code-Switching?

The ability to shift between languages, dialects, or communication styles depending on context.

### When to Code-Switch

- **Professional Settings**: Formal language, industry jargon
- **Casual Networking**: More relaxed, conversational tone
- **Client Meetings**: Match the client's communication style
- **Team Collaboration**: Adapt to team culture
- **Written vs. Spoken**: Different formality levels

## Module 8: Handling Language Challenges

### Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: Forgetting a word mid-conversation**
- Solution: Use the word in your other language and explain
- Example: "The word I'm looking for is... how do you say درآمد in English... revenue!"

**Challenge 2: Accent or pronunciation concerns**
- Solution: Embrace your accent—it's part of your unique brand
- Speak clearly and don't rush

**Challenge 3: Cultural misunderstandings**
- Solution: Ask for clarification immediately
- Example: "Just to make sure I understood correctly..."

**Challenge 4: Translating idioms**
- Solution: Explain the concept rather than translating directly
- Keep a list of equivalent expressions

## Module 9: Building Confidence in Both Languages

### Daily Practice Routines

**English Practice (15 minutes/day):**
- [ ] Read business news (Wall Street Journal, Forbes)
- [ ] Watch business presentations (TED Talks)
- [ ] Practice speaking to mirror or record yourself
- [ ] Write in English journal

**Farsi Practice (15 minutes/day):**
- [ ] Read Persian business articles
- [ ] Listen to Farsi podcasts/interviews
- [ ] Engage with Farsi-speaking community
- [ ] Practice formal and informal registers

**Bilingual Practice (15 minutes/day):**
- [ ] Translate business concepts between languages
- [ ] Practice your pitch in both languages
- [ ] Role-play conversations with language partner
- [ ] Write emails in both languages

### 30-Day Challenge

**Week 1: Foundation**
- Set up bilingual social media profiles
- Create business cards in both languages
- Update resume/CV in both languages

**Week 2: Practice**
- Attend one networking event using both languages
- Record yourself giving your pitch in both languages
- Connect with 5 people in each language

**Week 3: Implementation**
- Send 10 bilingual outreach emails
- Post bilingual content on social media
- Have 3 conversations switching between languages

**Week 4: Mastery**
- Lead a meeting or presentation bilingually
- Close a deal or project in your non-native language
- Teach someone something in both languages
