## Module 2: The Roots of Shyness

### Common Causes of Shyness

- **Cultural Background**: Growing up in environments where speaking up was discouraged
- **Past Experiences**: Negative reactions when expressing yourself
- **Fear of Judgment**: Worrying about what others think
- **Perfectionism**: Setting unrealistically high standards
- **Lack of Practice**: Limited opportunities to develop social skills

## Module 3: Building Your Assertiveness Foundation

### The Four Communication Styles

1. **Passive**: Avoiding conflict, not expressing needs
2. **Aggressive**: Expressing needs at the expense of others
3. **Passive-Aggressive**: Indirect expression of negative feelings
4. **Assertive**: Clear, respectful expression of needs and boundaries

### Assertiveness Formula

**"I feel ___________ when ___________ because ___________. I would like ___________."**

**Practice Examples:**

Situation 1: A friend frequently cancels plans at the last minute

"I feel disappointed when plans are cancelled last minute because I've arranged my schedule around them. I would like advance notice if you need to reschedule."

## Module 7: Handling Difficult Conversations

### The DESC Method

- **D**escribe the situation objectively
- **E**xpress how it makes you feel
- **S**pecify what you want to happen
- **C**onsequences (positive outcomes if change happens)

### Dealing with Criticism

**Healthy responses to criticism:**

1. **Listen fully** without interrupting
2. **Ask clarifying questions** if needed
3. **Acknowledge** what's valid
4. **Disagree respectfully** with what isn't
5. **Thank them** for their feedback

## Module 8: Building Social Confidence

### Conversation Starters

Practice these in low-stakes situations first:

- "What brought you here today?"
- "I love your [item], where did you get it?"
- "Have you been to [event/place] before?"
- "What do you do for work/fun?"
- "How do you know [host/organizer]?"

### Active Listening Skills

- Maintain eye contact
- Use verbal affirmations ("I see," "That makes sense")
- Ask follow-up questions
- Paraphrase to show understanding
- Avoid planning your response while they're speaking
